Writing Abstract

Key Components of an Effective Research Paper Abstract

What is Writing Abstract ?

An abstract is a brief summary of a scientific paper, such as a paper, thesis, or journal article. The main purpose of the abstract is to provide an overview of the content and contribution of the written work, so that the reader can quickly understand the essence of the work without having to read the whole document.

An abstract is a brief, comprehensive summary of the contents of the article; it allows readers to survey the contents of an article quickly and, like a title, it enables persons interested in the document to retrieve it from abstracting and indexing databases. Most scholarly journals require an abstract. Consult the instructions to authors or the web page of the journal to which you plan to submit your article for any journal-specific instructions. A well-prepared abstract can be the most important single paragraph in an article. Most people have their first contact with an article by seeing just the abstract, usually in comparison with several other abstracts, as they are doing a literature search. Readers frequently decide on the basis of the abstract whether to read the entire article. The abstract needs to be dense with information. By embedding keywords in your abstract, you enhance the user's ability to find it. Do not exceed the abstract word limit of the journal to which you are submitting your article. Word limits vary from journal to journal and typically range from 150 to 250 words.

A. Tips for Writing an Effective Abstract:

  • Be Concise: The abstract should be brief but informative. Avoid using overly long sentences or unnecessary details.
  • Use Clear and Concise Language: Choose precise words and avoid ambiguous or hard-to-understand phrases.
  • Follow a Clear Structure: Ensure the abstract follows a logical order, starting from the background, methods, findings, to implications.
  • Highlight Specific Contributions: Clearly explain the unique contributions or significant findings of the paper.
  • Revise and Improve: After writing the abstract, take time to revise and refine it to keep it concise and informative.


    • B. Example Abstract:

    Title: "The Impact of Global Warming on Biodiversity in Tropical Rainforests"

    Abstract:

    This study investigates the impact of global warming on biodiversity in tropical rainforests. Field survey methods were used to collect data on species variation of flora and fauna in several different tropical rainforest locations. Data analysis showed a significant decrease in species diversity, particularly in areas experiencing higher temperature increases. These findings highlight the importance of more serious conservation efforts in addressing the challenges of global warming on tropical rainforest ecosystems.

    Keywords : Global Warming;Biodiversity;Tropical Rainforests;Species Diversity;Conservation Efforts.
